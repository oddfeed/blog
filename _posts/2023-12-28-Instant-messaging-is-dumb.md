Instant messaging is dumb
=========================

Most messaging not only doesn't need to be instant but it being instant actually harms it.

  

Open loops
----------

If the brain has an incomplete task it tries to retain it for quick access, when is instant messaging ever a complete task? that's right it isn't, so for the entire work day your brain is trying to retain some aspect of your messaging, never letting go because the task is simply never done.

  

===

No benefit
----------

There is just no benefit to instant messaging, people will claim "oh it's good for not missing urgent things that you have to repsond to right away" This is a fair point but ultimately falls flat when you consider this makes up probably under 1% of message and for that 1% you can just @ them so the notification goes through (most platofrms allow granular notification control), you can gain all of the benefits of instant messaging with none of the distractions, none of the inhibited ability to focus, none of the constant noise, none of the time wasting.

  

What to do
==========

The system I use is actually incredibly simple and allows timely, well thought out responses

  

1.  Peiodically check messages (once first thing, once before lunch, once early afternoon)
2.  I read every message I recieve
3.  I spend probably under a minute figuring out which is most urgent to respond to
4.  I respond to the messages
5.  I forget about them all until the next check

  

  

* * *

Anything urgent can still come through with pings, nothing else can distract me, everyone gets a response and I get to remain focused on work.
