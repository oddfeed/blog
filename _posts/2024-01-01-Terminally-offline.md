b-but I can escape the algorithms

This is a myopic view, algorithms will always follow you. To use youtube for an example the main algorithmic portions are the homepage, recommended and next up, So you hide them. However the content creators you watch are created by those same algorithms because they're terminally online, they're not just informed by the algorithm they're rewarded for following the trends it sets, so now you're still consuming algorithmic content you're just one step away from the algorithm, Any content (on or off youtube) that is created by someone who is terminally online will follow this same pattern and almost all content online is created by people who're terminally online.

The solution

Some online content consumption is probably fine, but the more references it makes to online culture the worse it is. You need content that is incredibly focused on real world topics, I'm going to give two examples
1. A fitness youtube channel who gives advice and covers/reacts to the content of other fitness youtubers
2. A fitness youtube channel who makes videos about fitness
The example is a clear one but channels who do “react” content are always going to be informed by/influenced by internet culture to such a degree you'll get second hand influence, in my view the only way to overcome this is to be terminally offline

Terminally offline refers to being completely unaware and uninfluenced by algorithmic culture and internet trends. It is about distancing oneself from content pushed by algorithms and focusing on real-world topics instead. Algorithms can create inauthenticity and influence people's preferences, leading to the adoption of trends that may not align with their true interests or beliefs.
Escaping algorithms is difficult, as even content creators who hide algorithmic content are still influenced by it. Consuming content created by individuals who are terminally online will perpetuate the cycle of algorithmic influence.
To combat this, consume content that is focused on real-world topics and devoid of references to online culture.
Becoming terminally offline involves removing internet slang from one's vocabulary and examining likes and dislikes to determine if they were pushed on them by algorithms. The goal is to be completely unaware of online culture so that conversations about it hold no interest or relevance.
These steps may make one appear ignorant or socially awkward but argues that it allows for more meaningful conversations focused on real-life issues.
In summary, being terminally offline means disconnecting from algorithmic culture, focusing on real-world topics, and rejecting internet trends pushed by algorithms. The goal is to have genuine interests and beliefs uninfluenced by online culture.
