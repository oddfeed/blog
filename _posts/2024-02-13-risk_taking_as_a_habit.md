# Risk taking as a habit


<a id="org54db613"></a>

# Introduction

Due to luck and life circumstance I&rsquo;ve managed to meet and speak to a fair amount of highly driven and successful people, one habit or mindset I&rsquo;ven ot seen talked about is in relation to not only risk taking - but in creating additional risk.


<a id="org5e966fd"></a>

# Avoidable risk

Certain risks are absolutely avoidable but sometimes are not avoided, avoidable risks are

1.  Not dressing nicely
2.  Not being clean
3.  Not being prosocial

These things are risk because they&rsquo;ll lower your chance of success in almost everything you do, seriously. But you might opt to not avoid those risks because of other reasons, for example if you have strong insecurities about how you look purposefully exposing yourself to that insecurity can help overcome it - high success people assess not only risk but if the risk is even worth minimising, if you need to drive somewhere and it&rsquo;s raining the rain is a risk, but to minimise that risk you would wait until it stops raining, this is absurd. The point is not all risk is bad.


<a id="orgad35ad6"></a>

# Artifical risk

Artifical risk is more of a strategy I&rsquo;ve seen leverages where the person creates risk to either push things further or increase work capacity, examples are

1.  Adding deadlines with consequences if you don&rsquo;t meet them
2.  Increasing expectations
3.  Increasing scope

The motives and effect should be obvious, if you can run 5km on a treadmill before you can&rsquo;t run anymore get someone to point a gun to your head and shoot you if you run under 10km and you&rsquo;ll run 10km, it&rsquo;s not because you lied about only being able to run 5km it&rsquo;s that risk accutely increased your work capacity, but having too much risk can backfire.


<a id="org4359d1b"></a>

# Actual risk

Actual risk is risk that is inherent to the thing, this should be considered before starting, what are the unavoidable risks and how likely are they.

When you get in a car you accept the risk of crashing, however the risk is relatively low for the value provided - this type of consideration should be applied to as much as you can.
