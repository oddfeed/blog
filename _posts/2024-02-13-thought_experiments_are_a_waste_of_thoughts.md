## The purpose of thought experiments

This is going to be centered around the type of thought experiements often engaged in by decels like [here](https://twitter.com/GaryMarcus/status/1754239205776265667) - is a lot of these thought experiments are used either maliciously or stupidly to force the other person to make concessions that&rsquo;re actually irrelevant to the point this can best be seen in [beff jezos vs connor leahy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0zxi0xSBOaQ) Whenever a thought experiment is used and beff makes a concession it doesn&rsquo;t further anything because the thought experiment forces beff to have a different worldview than he has - but the purpose of thought experiments is to test your current worldview or beliefs since your beliefs are an extension of your worldview forcing someone to first change their worldview means you can no longer test/explore their beliefs. There will be edge cases but generally speaking making someone conceed their worldview with a thought experiment is just a waste.

the tweet I linked to has been reposted by a lot of people in the decel space as well as actual PHDs but lets as a thought experiment use the logic on that tweet and apply it to another topic

> A serious thought experiment for christians:

> If you had a time machine and could skip forward a decade and see that christianity has been disproven and caused serious harm to large numbers of people, either through massive religious persecution or the development and distribution of religiously motivated hatred, would you regret having been an christian?

it sounds utterly deranged.

## Constrained models

The second issue is the constraints usually give no worthwhile insight due to it reducing everything purely to logic but someones logic actually comes secondary to their action, theres research showing that if someoen is morally (in their mind logically) against cheating that actually has no bearing on if they cheat or not - my point being someones logic might give some indication of their actions in the real world but this isn&rsquo;t always (I would say isn&rsquo;t often) the case, a much more reliable predictor of behaviour is previous behaviour, if you rack your brain I&rsquo;m sure you&rsquo;ll reach the same conclusion previous actions are a much better predictor of future behaviour.

## Emotion

Leading on from that the sanatised thought experiment also disregards the persons actual emotions which are again a very important part of someones worldview, a lot of racist people argue in favor of racism in thought experiments for this exact reason, because they know their emotional hatred undermines their arguments and worldview, it shows a literal bias and the foundation they build on is emotion not logic, the logic seeks to justify or encourage the emotion, but if you only engage in sterilized thought experiments this becomes less clear.

## Capacity

The last two build into the main crux of the issue it completely disregards capacity, in a thought experiment everyone would (I hope) say and believe they wouldn&rsquo;t steal £10,000 from a close friend - but when the money and oppurtunity is there who knows what they would do, they don&rsquo;t and neither do you - &ldquo;oh but thought experiments are meant to test moral beliefs not actions&rdquo; a moral belief without action is just a thing we say to make ourselves sound good, it&rsquo;s worthless a moral belief is something you act upon, everything else is just noise.
