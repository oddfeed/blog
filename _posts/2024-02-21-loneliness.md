# What is loneliness?

Loneliness is a social pain, a type of pain that encourages social connections to help alleviate the pain, in all of the clinical literature chronic loneliness correlates strongly with poor physical and psychological outcomes and even suicide however even with this view being the overwhelming scientific consensus loneliness is still often romanticised most commonly in literature. With its hardships and destructive outcomes being seen as in some way beneficial to or necessary for development.

# Loneliness as experience

It begins to build, beneath my consciousness carefully extending until it has a grip upon me and then I feel it&rsquo;s grasp, the coldness sets in an uncomfortable sense becomes present in my mind then I identify it, I **feel** completely and utterly alone, disconnected from all who surround me it will never pass I have no connection to any of this, the connections that might have been present have been severed never to return; they simply allow my existence to interact with theirs they don&rsquo;t like me, it&rsquo;s all just tolerance, the thoughts take their form in mocking me until I can no longer bare it my head feels as though it might explode and I cannot remain a force I cannot see is crushing my body until I will cease to exist.

The inarticulate experience begins, I do not have anyone I am connected to, I see invisible lines and expressions in conversations that&rsquo;ve never been present with me, I see failures in communication and understanding that only exist within interactions with me - I feel alien, not from this earth and not a part of my physical species I **cannot** be human I can merely imitate one well enough to avoid suspicion but now the truth is presented to me and I cannot escape it - no resolution occurs just a gradual release of pressure inside my head until the experience has drawn to a close, the crying begins along with a more common experience of sadness, each time a residue is left behind, until it seeps beyond the experience of loneliness into all experience, it will end in me sinking into that sludge until I drown.

# Loneliness as a state of being

The reason loneliness is in my life so pervasive is because I exist within it, I&rsquo;m not sure if it has always been this way but I cannot remember another. The first time I can remember feeling like I wasn&rsquo;t alone was on psychedelic compounds, when I thought of my friends and family a new unfamiliar element was added, it can only be described as an alleviation of an existing feature not an experience as itself but a sudden lifting of weight, as if for the first time a hunchback is able to extend their spine fully and feel all the associated muscles finally engage as they should. I began using psychedelics to chase this alleviation and it worked until it not longer did, now my state was more hunched over than ever before.

as I exist within loneliness I cannot exist outside of it, the time for that has passed, I will only ever reside here until each time when it seeps into me causing the experience of loneliness each more intense than the last. Do people not feel it? the sludge around their ankles as they try to navigate the world, much like a burst of energy I&rsquo;ve found socialisation to allow me to forget or &rsquo;tune out&rsquo; the weight of that sludge, but afterwards the sludge gains ever so slightly in weight but I become much weaker, barely able to leave my bed due to the overwhelming weight, on nights after I socialise and get energised by it the only reason I sleep is because I cannot walk anymore, my eyelids become much too heavy on the verge of collapse I lie down and as always is the case the experience of loneliness begins though on some nights I&rsquo;m capable of falling asleep before it becomes too distressful on others it keeps me awake.

# The inability to be known

From loneliness comes obscurity, through a lack of experience or **felt** experience - Most can understand intuitively their feelings and the authenticity of them and to go a step further can even verbally express them with little effort, I don&rsquo;t believe any of this applies to me - if you were to survey all of my closest friends with whom I&rsquo;ve never been intentionally dishonest and asked them who I am as a person you would get drastically different answers, if I were to say what I&rsquo;m thinking about in a video and each friend were to watch it they would all draw different meaning from it, because I exist only through an obscurity and a diffused silhouette behind frosted glass a humanoid like figure can be made out and the observer often attributes features to that silhouette to make themselves more comfortable with looking at it but the image does not exist.

The possibility must be entertained that behind the glass is nothing, no human just a trick of lighting to give the impression of a human shape - from all I&rsquo;ve read it seems love may be the answer that someone will find a way to shatter the glass and I will be seen for the first time; maybe even guide me to a mirror so that I could see my own reflection but of all of this I&rsquo;m doubtful, relationships in my past only seek to distract me from the loneliness not offer resolution to it, upon what I&rsquo;ve read it seems as if resolutions are a matter of luck more than anything else.

# The self feeding cycle

I cannot attribute any compliments about who I am to myself, I feel no firm attachment to my identity and more importantly I do not believe anyone knows me better than myself, now if that is the case and I (a) Do not know myself and (b) Do not like the things I do know about myself than any compliment for who I am must be a sign of my dishonesty, if I were acting honestly their conclusions about me must be identical to mine or their conclusions must be based in fiction - they don&rsquo;t know me or I&rsquo;ve been dishonest about who I am seem to be the only possibilities both of which force the experience of loneliness to occur for what should be fairly obvious reasons, I&rsquo;m either not known or I&rsquo;m in some capacity deceiving the people I care about, both of which push me further away from those I care about causing the feelings of loneliness to increase.
