# I don&rsquo;t sleep

> I don&rsquo;t sleep, I just dream

I don&rsquo;t usually relate to lines from media but this line from true detective season 1 has stuck with me for some time, because even though it&rsquo;s technically incorrect that doesn&rsquo;t stop it from being true - a statement that is both false and true, I think that&rsquo;s why it stuck around in my head, it was only after I said something similar to a friend that I thought more about it. I looked terrible and they asked me how I&rsquo;ve been sleeping and I responded with I don&rsquo;t really sleep I just lay still and hallucinate for a few hours, I said it without thinking but it felt true, it&rsquo;s lead to me taking things a lot less literally because language is more about vibes than anything else.

Sleep implies several things

1.  A feeling of being refreshed
2.  Lessening of tiredness
3.  Relaxation

but you can meet the scientific criteria of sleep without any of these 3 being met but when someone asks if you slept they aren&rsquo;t asking about changes in your brain state, temperature etc.
this is evident by how common &ldquo;I didn&rsquo;t sleep at all last night is&rdquo;

I didn&rsquo;t sleep implies that last night, the 3 criteria aren&rsquo;t met when they normally are - it illustrates an outlier.

I don&rsquo;t sleep implies the same but as a long standing and ongoing trend.

This leads me to double down on my instinctive response, I don&rsquo;t really sleep I just hallucinate for a few hours - most of the time I wake up feeling worse than when I went to sleep, I&rsquo;ve long struggled with night terrors that lead me to quite intense stimulant use to minimise sleep which means I&rsquo;m very aware of my dreams as they&rsquo;re happening and I remember them vividly, both of which make them (not literally) clsoer to hallucinations than dreams - in my view every part of this statement is false and true, it more accurately communicates my subjective experiance.
