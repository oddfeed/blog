# Slow drip poison

Socialisation is a foundational part of what it means to be human, it is non negotiable - community is one of the only things outside of survival we can say with certainty matters, people who self ascribe as introverts are engaging in a form of self harm - they&rsquo;re otherising themselves from prosocial people, each person has a different level of socialisation they wish to engage in but this should never result in distinct and exclusionary catagories - do not define yourself by the fact other people want to socialise more or less than you or you&rsquo;ll enter into an infinite fracturing a self fufilling profecy that leads to self destruction - to a lack of humanity. I preface this to avoid this getting misconstrued as me being an introvert or anti social. I am very prosocial and extroverted.

I want to document my experiance of socialisation, it should first be said since I live in a poor area socialisation is mostly isolated to drinking in the pub, I do not drink or do drugs but I still enjoy pubs and clubs, I enjoy seeing and talking to my friends, meeting new people and the small talk that comes with it. I feel energised, invigorated as though every single synapse in my brain is firing, my brain is suddenly switched on, thinking becomes effortless as does enjoying myself.

This typically continues until the early morning, as it comes to a close I feel an anxiety looming, a sadness building because I know what comes next, it comes after any social event, the dread, the loneliness, the feeling of despair, hopelessness - it always comes crashing onto me like a tidal waves washing me away from the people I care about stranding me to isolation.

For this reason I always opt to walk home alone, rain, cold it doesn&rsquo;t make a difference I don&rsquo;t want to be around anyone I don&rsquo;t want to spend 20 minutes sat in an uber even if it&rsquo;s with a stranger I don&rsquo;t want to be in anyones proximity I just want to cease, to vanish, to be allowed to dissolve into nothingness. Each time I walk home I say I will feel better in the morning but it&rsquo;s never the case, I feel progressively worse physically and mentally for the following days.

This is even seen in my biometrics, I can&rsquo;t sleep and when I do I have fragmented vivid dreams that make me feel even worse than not sleeping at all, my HRV drops for several days, my resting heart rate increases for several days and I get almost like flu symptoms, a headache that doesn&rsquo;t pass, an increase in my bodies temperature, as though my immune system is trying to fight my emotional state which is one of depression.

I will spend the next few days barely able to catch a thought, no control over my mind, a restlessness that makes me pace until my legs are sore, an inability to do anything, I can&rsquo;t think, I can&rsquo;t focus, I can&rsquo;t sleep, I can&rsquo;t enjoy anything, food tastes bland, nothing has color and my eyes can&rsquo;t focus on anything. There is no relief you just have to wait, it&rsquo;ll pass then the need to socialise builds until the cycle repeats.
