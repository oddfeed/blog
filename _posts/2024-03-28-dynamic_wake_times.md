# Dynamic wake times


<a id="org8fe11ed"></a>

## Issues with sleep

I&rsquo;ve longstanding had issues with my sleep, struggling to sleep, sleeping too little or of insufficiant quality, meaning I&rsquo;ve tried a lot of things and this is the only thing that has worked consistently overtime - it is not perfect and my sleep is still worse than I would like it to be as detailed in other blog posts like [I dont sleep](https://blog.alienate.earth/i_dont_sleep/) but this system still offers practical value in both it&rsquo;s simplicity (set an alarm to go to sleep but not to wake up) and it&rsquo;s application (can be done even when you have to wake up at a certain time range)


<a id="orga21c5d1"></a>

## wake/sleep time as regulators

I want ot quickly note that our sleep wake cycles are regulated by a lot of things, food, social contact, blue light, the media we consume, when we exercise etc, but it&rsquo;s more strongly set by going to sleep at the same time each day compared to waking up at the same time each day.


<a id="org38037fe"></a>

## Common sense

If we stop and apply common sense to the idea of waking up at the same time each day it quickly falls apart - The ammount of sleep we need obviously varies drastically but the time we get tired doesn&rsquo;t correlate with that, if I normally wake up at 8am and I normally need 8 hours of sleep, if one day I need 9 hours I might not know that I need 9 hours and more importantly that doesn&rsquo;t mean I&rsquo;d get tired at 11pm instead of midnight so on that day I&rsquo;d likely get 8 hours when I need nine - or I could get 8 hours when I only needed 7.


<a id="orgb4c7ded"></a>

# My approach

My personal approach is to figure out what range of sleep I need, I need a maximum of 9 hours (though most days much less) so if I want to wake up at 8am I need to make sure each night I&rsquo;m asleep by 11pm then whatever time I wake up at I get up at now when I say &ldquo;wake up&rdquo; I don&rsquo;t mean it exactly if I wake up at 2am to piss I&rsquo;m not going to stay up I&rsquo;m going to go back to sleep, I mean times in which you wake up naturally - the aclimation is strange but in the long run it seems much better, I&rsquo;ve had several nights where I&rsquo;ve naturally woke up after 4-5 hours of sleep and been perfectly fine the next day - now when I say fine I mean relative to my historic trends not by the standards of medical professionals

This makes much more intuitive sense since the amount of sleep we need we will naturally try to achieve once asleep (i.e days where you ignore your alarm) and usually when we need more sleep we don&rsquo;t go to bed earlier we just want ot wake up later - it actually gives you body a chance to self regulate - I&rsquo;d also guess on some level your body need to be asleep to know how much sleep you need since how much sleep you need would also vary based on quality and quality is unknown until you actually are sleeping.
