# The medium you learn from is no neutral

An increasing trend of people learning from youtube videos is causing great harm to people (5bathrooms) As people seek to become smarterer they will increasing fall into the same traps, I&rsquo;m going to talk about two mediums (books and youtube) this isn&rsquo;t to say one is better than the other because I don&rsquo;t believe one is, if you find books incredibly boring your time would be better spent learning from something else, I&rsquo;m doing this to illustrate concepts that can be applied elsewhere

# Cost of production

If I want to make a book up until very recently (chatgpt) it was costly in every sense of the word, it would take a lot of time, a lot of effort and would have a high chance of failure since self publishing is still not incredibly viable especially for education based books - not only that but in every education based book publishers care about the author thats why they will credential dump and give you a history of the author that serves to make them seem credible - for a youtube video almost none of this is the case, you can make a youtube video about any topic in a matter of hours.

# Types of consumers

This point is short and sweet, if a book becomes successful it gets more people reviewing and fact checking it, with youtube videos this is often not the case because of (a) culture and (b) the amount of youtube videos not only being made but the amount of youtube videos being made and going viral makes it so that there isn&rsquo;t enough reviewers to actually address it.

# Snopes fact check (tm)

Ask yourself, if you&rsquo;re learning something by default you have to be watching content about things you don&rsquo;t know, and if you don&rsquo;t know fact checking them becomes incredibly difficult - If you have to fact check everything the amount of effort and time learning takes increases drastically.

I&rsquo;m not saying never fact check I&rsquo;m saying not being able to trust any of the information is much more common on a low cost video that&rsquo;s meant ot fit a mass production scheme, think about how many education channels can somehow make educational videos on every single topic, it would have you believe that polymaths are a dime a dozen, whats much more likely is you can just spew a lot of misinfo on youtube without it mattering.

&ldquo;oh but the comments will correct it&rdquo; this isn&rsquo;t true at all, someone who&rsquo;s educationed on topic x and someone who&rsquo;s learning about topic x are not watching the same videos, the overlap doesn&rsquo;t exist. So now you&rsquo;re back to having to double check any and all major claims, 2xing if not more the amount of time and effort it will take.

# How you engage

Books require active engagement, videos don&rsquo;t, I cannot read a book whilst playing video games, I can watch educational youtube videos, but if you&rsquo;re doing this you should consider it entertainment and not learning, learning is deliberate, intentional and planned out ahead of time, it isn&rsquo;t passively listening to words as you play league of legends. Each medium will encourage and discourage types of engagement but you can choose to watch the youtube video on 1x speed, fullscreen whilst writing notes.

# What to do

Heres the part where I mostly shill for institutions because they&rsquo;re checking who produces the content, they&rsquo;re also held to a much higher level of scrutiny, MIT, Stanford and even harvard upload lectures to youtube. In summary

1.  Who&rsquo;s making the content?
2.  Are they an expert or generalist?
3.  What checks exist to make sure they&rsquo;re accurate?
4.  What consequences do they face if they&rsquo;re wrong?
5.  Are they incentivised to get views or educate people?
6.  Look for something they&rsquo;ve made on a topic you know a lot about then critically review it
